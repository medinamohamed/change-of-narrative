Our Mission

This project seeks to identify and celebrate the contributions of young Black, Indigenous and Women of Colour (BIWOC), including queer and trans women, towards social justice and fostering inclusion, equity, and transformation in their communities. Starting with the premise that BIWOC have long been at the forefront of promoting justice and equity in Quebec, and that their contributions have been largely under­recognized and overlooked, this research uses feminst oral history methods to “change the narrative” on what constitutes a “leader” or “hero.” This project focusses on the everyday actions and engagements of BIWOC youth who are committed to anti-racist and anti-colonial work. By publicly celebrating and enhancing the visibility of their accomplishments, we are looking to know: who are the BIWOC youth promoting equity at this time in Quebec? And what is their vision of equity and social justice.
